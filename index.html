My recent work:

1. Abelytics.io

Abelytics is a platform for teachers to gather data from students, visualise and export the resulting analyses, and send personalised homework to students. Data collection is simplified through Abel; visualizations are a click away; recommendations are provided to teachers on what to teach; homework is personalised according to student weaknesses, at a click of a button.

Put together with a PEAN stack - Postgres, Express.js, Angular 8, Node.js and with some MongoDB - I built and deployed the entire web-app end-to-end: authentication flow using JWTs, complex reactive forms in Angular, database schema architecting, to API integrations with Sendgrid and AWS S3.

Some of the more interesting aspects relate to matching student weakness with the homework generated by Abel. To do so, I used a time-weighted scale to score student's mastery of a given topic, and matched it to a bank of past year questions manually indexed. Currently, there are over 1000 distinct questions in the bank as ready material to be assigned to students.

To cater for multiple schools, I went with a schema-level multi-tenant approach, with the database hosted on AWS RDS. The work also involved maintaining nginx on the EC2 instance: cron jobs, letsencrypt SSL certificates.

Words from a student who used Abelytics in her Physics class to prepare for her revision: "I wish I had this in Chemistry, Biology.... Maths, Add Maths.. I wish this was in all my sciences!"
